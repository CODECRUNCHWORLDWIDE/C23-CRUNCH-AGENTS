# Week 1 — Challenges

The exercises drill the mechanics. **The challenge makes you the engineer in the room when the team has to pick a model.** You're handed three contrasting jobs and a fixed set of candidate models, and you have to choose one model per job and defend each choice with measured numbers — the way it happens in a real architecture review.

## Index

1. **[Challenge 1 — The model-selection memo](challenge-01-model-selection-memo.md)** — three jobs (a cheap high-volume classifier, a long-document summarizer, a hard reasoning task), one shortlist of candidate models, and a one-page memo per job that picks a model and backs it with measured latency, measured cost, and a license/data note. (~90 min)

Challenges are optional for passing the week, but this one is the single best preparation for the Phase I capstone milestone, where you defend your stack's model choices. It's also the closest thing in the course to a real "design a model-routing layer" interview question, which you will get if you apply for an applied-AI-engineer role. Do it. The skill — choosing a model under explicit constraints and defending it with numbers, not vibes — is exactly what separates a junior who "has used the API" from an engineer who can own a production decision.
